\section{Results}
\subsection{Analytic sample}
The primary cohort contained 14,582 eligible player-day pairs from 50 players. Team A contributed 3,812 pairs in 2020 and 5,881 in 2021, while Team B contributed 2,406 and 2,483, respectively (Table~\ref{tab:sample}). Across all primary pairs, both current-day and next-day readiness had a median of 7.0 (interquartile range 6.0--8.0). The median daily load was 350 session-RPE units (interquartile range 20--600), and 75.0\% of eligible pairs occurred on days with non-zero training load. The full calendar panel contained 36,550 player-days; exclusions arose primarily from missing current or next-day readiness rather than missing 7-day load history (Figure~\ref{fig:flow}; Supplementary Table~S5).

\subsection{Primary incremental-value comparison}
When the complete deployed pipeline was tuned separately within source-team 2020 data, all comparators selected $\alpha=1000$ from the prespecified candidate grid (Supplementary Table~S1). Under the primary 28-observation sequential residual window, \Pone{} did not improve MAE over \Pzero{} in any evaluation setting (Table~\ref{tab:primary}; Figure~\ref{fig:incremental}). Within Team A, \Pzero{} and \Pone{} had MAEs of 0.717 and 0.724, respectively (\DeltaMAE{} +0.007, 95\% CI $-$0.005 to +0.023). Within Team B, MAE was 0.619 for \Pzero{} and 0.637 for \Pone{} (\DeltaMAE{} +0.018, $-$0.005 to +0.047). In cross-team evaluation, \DeltaMAE{} was +0.022 ($-$0.005 to +0.053) from Team A to Team B and +0.004 ($-$0.007 to +0.016) from Team B to Team A. All four intervals crossed zero, and point estimates were small on the 1--10 readiness scale. Thus, the evaluated full panel showed no stable evidence of incremental MAE improvement beyond the same personal readiness-history information.

\subsection{Sensitivity analyses and calibration}
Changing the sequential residual window from 14 to 56 earlier eligible forecasts did not reveal a stable MAE advantage for \Pone{} (Figure~\ref{fig:window}; Supplementary Table~S2). The reduced monitoring model \Ptwo{} also did not demonstrate an incremental MAE advantage over \Pzero{} (Supplementary Table~S2).

The missingness-aware sensitivity used 14,631 player-day pairs with observed current and next-day readiness and 7-day load history. It used source-training median imputation and wellness-specific missingness indicators. Estimated \DeltaMAE{} values for the missingness-aware full model were +0.008 ($-$0.004 to +0.025), +0.020 ($-$0.006 to +0.049), +0.024 ($-$0.003 to +0.059), and +0.004 ($-$0.006 to +0.016) across the four settings, respectively (Supplementary Figure~S2; Supplementary Table~S7). Thus, broader eligibility and missingness indicators did not reveal stable incremental improvement.

Linear calibration slopes ranged from 0.908 to 0.928 for \Pzero{} and from 0.918 to 0.920 for \Pone{} under the primary window, indicating some disagreement between the spread of predictions and observations under the stated diagnostic (Supplementary Figure~S3; Supplementary Table~S3). Player-level \Pone{}--\Pzero{} MAE differences varied within each setting (Supplementary Figure~S4).
